# CCEC Climate Platform — Vietnam Climate Knowledge Base

## Overview
Vietnam has a tropical monsoon climate, strongly influenced by the East Asian monsoon system. The country spans from 23°N to 8°N latitude, creating significant regional climate variation.

---

## Regional Climate Characteristics

### Northern Vietnam (Red River Delta + Northern Mountains)
- **Climate type:** Sub-tropical, with cool winters
- **Temperature:** 15–37°C (winter: 10–20°C, summer: 28–37°C)
- **Rainfall:** 1,500–2,000 mm/year
- **Seasonality:** 4 distinct seasons; heavy rain Jun–Sep
- **Typhoon exposure:** High (8–10 typhoons/season, Aug–Oct)
- **Key hazards:** Flooding, typhoons, cold waves (偶有寒潮)

### Central Vietnam (North Central + South Central Coast)
- **Climate type:** Tropical monsoon with strong dry season
- **Temperature:** 20–40°C
- **Rainfall:** 1,500–3,000 mm/year (high at Annamite Range)
- **Typhoon exposure:** Very high — central coast is most typhoon-prone region
- **Key hazards:** Drought (Mar–Aug), typhoons, floods, landslides
- **Special:** The "Typhoon Belt" of Vietnam — Da Nang, Quang Nam, Thua Thien-Hue

### Central Highlands (Tay Nguyen)
- **Climate type:** Tropical savanna, high altitude
- **Temperature:** 18–30°C (cooler than lowlands)
- **Rainfall:** 1,500–2,500 mm/year
- **Seasonality:** Wet season May–Oct, dry season Nov–Apr
- **Key hazards:** Drought, forest fires (dry season), flash floods

### Southern Vietnam (Southeast + Mekong Delta)
- **Climate type:** Tropical savanna, monsoonal
- **Temperature:** 22–35°C year-round (minimal variation)
- **Rainfall:** 1,500–2,200 mm/year; monsoon Jun–Nov
- **Key hazards:** Flooding (Mekong Delta), saltwater intrusion, subsidence
- **Special:** Two seasons — wet (May–Nov) and dry (Dec–Apr)

## Key Climate Statistics

### Temperature Trends (1990–2023)
| Region | Avg Temp 1990 | Avg Temp 2023 | Warming |
|--------|--------------|---------------|---------|
| North  | 22.8°C      | 23.8°C        | +1.0°C  |
| Central| 26.1°C      | 27.2°C        | +1.1°C  |
| South  | 27.4°C      | 28.3°C        | +0.9°C  |

### Sea Level Rise (Vietnam Coast)
- Average rise: ~3.1 mm/year (1993–2023)
- Mekong Delta: up to 4–5 mm/year (highest subsidence in Can Tho, Bac Lieu)
- Projected 2050: +30 to +60 cm (RCP 4.5–8.5)

### Typhoon Statistics
- Average: 6–8 tropical storms/typhoons per year enter Vietnamese waters
- Most intense recorded: Typhoon DMCI (2020) — Category 5 equivalent
- Economic damage: ~1–3% GDP per major typhoon

## Climate Policy Documents

### National Strategy
1. **Vietnam Nationally Determined Contribution (NDC) 2022**
   - Reduction target: 15.8% GHG vs BAU by 2030 (conditional on intl support: 43.5%)
   - Key sectors: Energy, agriculture, LULUCF, waste
   - Unconditional: renewable energy expansion, afforestation

2. **National Climate Change Strategy (2021–2030, vision to 2050)**
   - Adaptation: agriculture, water resources, coastal zones
   - Mitigation: energy transition, green growth
   - Goals: net-zero by 2050 (pledged at COP26)

3. **National Adaptation Plan (NAP) 2021**
   - Priority: agriculture, water, coastal zones, health
   - Local-level adaptation planning

4. **Vietnam Green Growth Strategy (VGGS) 2021–2030**
   - GHG intensity reduction: 15% by 2030 vs 2020
   - Renewable energy: 30–35% by 2030
   - Green city development

### Key Regulations
- **Law on Environmental Protection 2020** (QCVN) — climate change integration
- **Law on Water Resources 2012** (amended 2023)
- **Law on Dykes and Dam** — coastal protection
- **Forest Protection Fund** — REDD+ implementation

## CCEC Platform Data Requirements

### Real-time Data Needed
1. **Weather:** Temperature, humidity, rainfall, wind speed/direction, pressure
2. **River:** Water level, discharge at major stations (Red River, Mekong, Thu Bon, Ba, etc.)
3. **Coastal:** Tide level, wave height, storm surge
4. **Air Quality:** PM2.5, AQI (major cities)
5. **Satellite:** Himawari-8/9 IR imagery for typhoon tracking

### Alert Thresholds (Reference)
- **Typhoon Warning:** Wind ≥ 62 km/h → Red alert
- **Flood Warning:** River level > 3m above danger level (Red River)
- **Heavy Rain:** ≥ 100 mm/24h → Orange alert; ≥ 200 mm/24h → Red
- **Heat Wave:** ≥ 35°C for 3+ days → Orange; ≥ 38°C → Red
- **Cold Wave:** ≤ 10°C in lowlands, ≤ 5°C in mountains → Orange
